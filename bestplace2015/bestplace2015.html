"STUDIO CENTER Named As One Of The
"BEST PLACES TO WORK IN VIRGINIA"
For The THIRD Consecutive Year"

Virginia Beach, VA — December 4, 2014.  Studio Center was recently named as one of the 2015 Best Places to Work in Virginia for the third straight year. Virginia Business and Best Companies Group created the annual list of the Best Places to Work in Virginia.

This statewide survey and awards program is designed to identify, recognize and honor the best places of employment in Virginia. The 2015 Best Places to Work in Virginia List is made up of the 100 very best companies.

To be considered for participation, companies had to fulfill the following eligibility requirements:

  • Be a for-profit business, a non-profit business or government entity
  • Be a publicly or privately held business
  • Have a facility in the state of Virginia
  • Have at least 15 employees working in Virginia
  • Be in business a minimum of 1 year

Thousands of companies from across the state entered the two-part survey process to determine the Best Places to Work in Virginia. The first part consisted of evaluating each nominated company's workplace policies, practices, philosophy, systems and demographics. This part of the process was worth approximately 25% of the total evaluation. The second part consisted of an employee survey to measure the employee experience. This part of the process was worth approximately 75% of the total evaluation. The combined scores determined the top companies and the final ranking. Best Companies Group managed the overall registration and survey process in Virginia and also analyzed the data and used their expertise to determine the final ranking.

For more information on the Best Places to Work in Virginia program, visit www.BestPlacesToWorkVA.com.

“This is HUGE News for Studio Center. We’re honored and humbled to have won for a third consecutive year. It was our goal 11 years ago to build a company where people would WANT to work. Thanks to my staff, Virginia Business and Best Companies Group for this award.” said William “Woody” Prettyman, Studio Center CEO.
